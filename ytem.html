Case Study 03

Ytem

Inventory management system for retail store operators — designed for the shop floor, not the office.

Role
UX & UI Designer
Type
Client project
Status
Deployed
Platform
Mobile + RFID
Context

Inventory management sounds like a back-office problem.

In practice, it's a real-time operational challenge — non-technical staff, variable lighting, physical tasks, and time pressure. The design had to work in that environment, not around it.

A retail technology client needed a faster, smarter way for store owners to manage inventory. Ytem was designed to solve this — a mobile app connected to a handheld RFID scanning device that automated inventory counting and fed that data directly into supply chain decisions.

The Problem

Inventory that lies to you.

Retail store owners were losing time and money to inaccurate inventory. The gap between what was physically on shelves and what the system recorded was creating supply chain failures — over-ordering, stock-outs, and missed sales.

The constraint that shaped every decision: the user is holding a scanner, standing in a stockroom, under time pressure. This is the same design problem as an operator dashboard in a high-stakes B2B environment — reduce cognitive load, surface what matters, get out of the way.

System Architecture

Four layers, one connected system.

Mobile app, RFID scanners, connection gateway, and data dashboard — connected into a single operational flow from physical scan to supply chain decision.

Design Decision 01

Dark UI for a bright shop floor.

The most important decision was the dark UI. Working in a retail environment meant screens needed to be easy to read under variable lighting conditions — the dark theme significantly improved scan visibility and reduced eye strain during extended use.

Design Decision 02

Three taps. Scan. Done.

The app was structured around the core scanning workflow — receive, scan, sync. Every screen was optimized to minimize taps and keep the user focused on the physical task at hand. Progress indicators, found/expected counters, and location labels gave operators constant awareness without overwhelming them.

Brand System

A visual identity built for dark environments.

Dark background, red accent for critical actions, clean typography optimized for small screens under variable light.

Design Principles

Three principles guided every screen.

01
Glanceable over readable
Operators process status at a distance — not sitting at a desk reading sentences.
02
Physical context first
One hand is always on the scanner. Every tap was designed around that constraint.
03
Zero training required
The flow had to be self-evident on day one, to someone who had never seen the app before.
Impact

Deployed to real retail environments.

Ytem was deployed to real retail environments and is actively used by store owners for inventory management. The product successfully bridged the gap between physical stock counting and digital supply chain management, reducing the friction of a previously manual and error-prone process.
